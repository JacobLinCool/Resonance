# Resonance Privacy Policy

Effective July 16, 2026

Resonance does not collect personal data, operate developer-controlled servers,
include analytics or advertising SDKs, or require a Resonance account.

## Microphone

When you enable Resonance, it accesses the microphone to identify music playing
nearby. Audio is processed in memory and supplied to Apple's ShazamKit for
recognition. Resonance does not create or retain audio recordings, and it does
not send microphone audio to servers controlled by the developer. Microphone
access stops while a matched song is playing and when you disable or quit the
app.

## Apple Music

Resonance requests access to Apple Music so it can check playback eligibility,
look up the identified catalog song, and play it at the synchronized position.
It does not read your playlists, favorites, or listening history. Use of
ShazamKit, MusicKit, and Apple Music is subject to
[Apple's Privacy Policy](https://www.apple.com/legal/privacy/).

## Data stored on your Mac

Your loudness threshold and sync adjustment are stored locally in Resonance's
app container. They are not transmitted to the developer. Resonance retains no
personal data on developer-controlled systems, so there is no developer-held
account or personal data to delete.

You can revoke Microphone or Media & Apple Music access at any time in
**System Settings > Privacy & Security**. You can remove locally stored settings
by deleting Resonance's app data.

## Changes and contact

Material changes to this policy will be published in this file with a revised
effective date. For privacy questions or support, open an issue in the
[Resonance repository](https://github.com/JacobLinCool/Resonance/issues).
